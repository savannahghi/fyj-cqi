DQA INSTRUCTIONS
OBJECTIVE

The objective of this DQA tool is to review and assess a facility's data management and reporting system to determine if the system is able to produce reports with good data quality and to verify that the data reported by the facility meets the national required standards. The tool will also be used to verify weekly & Monythly reports submitted by a facility during the RRI period.

CONTENT

The DQA tool comprises 4 sections:

  1. Summary Dashboard
  2. Systems Assessment
  3. Data Verification
  4. DQA Action plan

Each section should be completed to allow a complete outlook of the facility's perfomance. System Assessment is arranged into five sections:

  1. Organisation of M&E Structure and Functions
  2. Data Management Processes
  3. Indicator Definitions and Reporting Guidelines
  4. Data-collection and Reporting Forms / Tools
  5. EMR Systems

These assessment areas are critical to evaluating whether the Data Management and Reporting System is able to produce quality data. Questions related to the five areas are asked at different levels of the reporting system.

Data Verification comprise of select indicators reported through the National Reporting System (KHIS).

Verification of the data is to be done through a recount of primary data sources in this case registers and EMR if available.

Prevention data RRI verification looks at the reportable Prevention indicators where verification will be done against the reported monthly data.

Facility DQA Action Plan allows for all the issues noted in the exercise to be documented with clear action points, timelines and the responsible staff. The period of assessment will be as directed by the data manager.

DATA COLLECTION

Each facility should have its own data entry in system assessment, data verification and Facility DQA action plan filled. The data should be entered with care. Data verification should be filled with results of the recount from registers, data reported in MOH 731, data reported in KHIS and data reported in DATIM. Data from KHIS and DATIM can be prepopulated before the start of the exercise. The Facility DQA Action Plan sheet should be utilized to highlight the various strengths and gaps noted during the exercise and the agreed action plan with the facility.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

Corrective actions after the DQA are very important and should be worked on immediately. The action Plan template will be utilized for documentation and follow up of the same. Any facility scoring a red i.e <1.5 in any component of the system assessment should be earmarked for immediate remedial action. The FYJMEO should take charge of this together with the SCHRIO. There should be 0% margin of error for the following indicators: HTS_POS, PMTCT_POS, Maternal HAART, Infant Prophylaxis, TX_NEW, IPT_NEW. An allowable margin of error of +-3% will be accepted for TX_CURR. If the validated data goes beyond the allowable margin the facility report and submitted reports should be corrected immediately. The facility HRIO should take charge of reconstructing the reports in the MOH 731. The FYJMEO staff should submit corrected reports within a week to Data Management Unit, the SCHRIO should update KHIS within 2 weeks post DQA.
