Welcome to the LabPulse User Guide! This comprehensive guide is designed to help you learn and make the most of the powerful LabPulse app. It is a Django-based platform developed to improve and speed up the process of delivering CD4, reflex sCrAg and TB LAM results for patients in Nairobi and Kajiado.

1.1 What LabPulse Does

The App aims to bridge the gap in the timely delivery of services to patients with advanced HIV disease (AHD). It provides a seamless process for delivering CD4, reflex sCrAg and TB LAM results. Laboratory staff can easily upload results to the platform, and facility clinicians can access the results virtually. Cutting down the time it takes to deliver results empowers healthcare providers to take prompt and, targeted action to help patients in need.

1.2 How LabPulse Improves AHD Screening and Management

LabPulse offers a range of advantages that make AHD screening and management more efficient and effective:

  1. Quicker Results Delivery: The most important benefit LabPulse offers is significantly speeding up the time it takes to get results from the lab to the clinician. This allows quicker treatment decisions and better outcomes for patients.
  2. User-Friendly Design: LabPulse has an intuitive, easy-to-use interface that simplifies navigation and data entry, even for users with minimal tech skills. This smoothens the workflows and boosts productivity.
  3. Comprehensive Data Entry: The app enables entry of both current and past results, giving complete patient records. This comprehensive approach simplifies data management and access.
  4. Metrics and Visualizations: LabPulse presents key metrics and visualizations like CD4 distributions, positivity rates, and weekly trends. These visual aids provide valuable insights to inform healthcare professionals.
  5. Future Reagent Tracking: Upcoming versions of LabPulse will include reagent tracking to enhance lab inventory management for uninterrupted testing.
1.3 Who LabPulse is For

LabPulse serves a range of essential roles in AHD management. Here’s a quick look at how different users benefit:

  1. Clinicians : As frontline healthcare providers, clinicians need access to the most up-to-date CD4 and sCrAg results to make informed patient treatment decisions. LabPulse empowers clinicians by delivering quick access to critical lab results to effectively tailor care plans and monitor disease progression.
  2. Laboratory Staff: Laboratory technicians play a crucial behind-the-scenes role in conducting laboratory tests and generating results. For them, LabPulse simplifies workflows by providing an intuitive interface to log and transmit CD4, sCrAg and TB LAM data to clinicians seamlessly.
  3. Facility Managers and HIV implementing partners: LabPulse offers invaluable oversight into operations. It allows easy access to patient testing data, helps monitor lab throughput, and informs how to allocate resources for optimal results.
  4. County and Sub-county Leads: For health authorities at county and sub-county levels, LabPulse enables real-time tracking and management of AHD screening and care across facilities in their network. The data in LabPulse empowers leads to make well-informed decisions and strategically guide screening and treatment programs.
1.4 Getting Login Credentials

To access LabPulse, users must get login credentials from the platform administrator. Provide your email and carder. The administrator will review and grant you access to the App. In the following sections, we’ll guide you through all the App’s features to help you get the most out of this powerful tool. Let’s get started!

2. Getting Started
{% elif section == "getting_started" %}

Welcome to LabPulse, an advanced platform that enhances AHD screening and patient management. This guide will walk you through the initial steps to access the application and establish your user account.

2.1 Obtaining Login Credentials

To start using LabPulse, you will need login credentials provided by the platform administrator. Please get in touch with the administrator by filling out the access request form , indicating your name, email and designation to request credentials if you have not yet received them. Labpulse is hosted on: https://cqi.fahariyajamii.org/

Note: A stable internet connection is required to utilize LabPulse effectively. Before login, ensure you have reliable connectivity.

2.2 First Login and Profile Setup

Upon logging in for the first time, you will be directed to set up your user profile. Completing your profile is essential to access all functionality relevant to your user role.

During setup, you will be asked to enter:

  • First and last name
  • Contact phone number
  • Email address

You may opt to change your username at this stage if desired. Be sure to retain your username for future logins.

2.3 Navigating the Application

After completing your profile, you can access the main dashboard. Key sections include:

  • Instructions : Usage guidelines and documentation
  • Data Entry : For entry of CD4 results, current and retrospective
  • Results: View and analyze all CD4 results, sCrAg and TB LAM. Custom filters are available.

And that covers the basics to get you rolling with LabPulse! In the sections ahead, we’ll explore all the sections in detail. Let’s go - your journey with LabPulse starts now!

3. Entering Results
{% elif section == "entering_results" %}

The CD4 Results Entry section allows you to efficiently record current and historical CD4 and reflex sCrAg results for patients , enabling timely and accurate data management. This section consists of Ongoing CD4 Entry and Retrospective CD4 Entry.

3.1 Entering Current CD4 Results

In this section, you can easily log patients’ latest CD4, reflex sCrAg, and TB LAM results as soon as they become available from the testing lab.

Just follow these straightforward steps:

  1. Select the appropriate testing lab from the dropdown menu for which you will enter results.
  2. Enter essential patient information, including facility name, unique ID, age, and gender. These details are vital for proper patient identification and result tracking.
  3. Enter the CD4 count and percentage values in the designated fields. If reflex sCrAg testing was done, also enter the sCrAg result.
  4. Enter the collection date, sample collection date, and testing date. The system will automatically log the dispatch date.
  5. Provide additional relevant details, such as reasons for rejection, not conducting sCrAg testing, TB LAM result, and CD4 % values.

Please note that fields like facility, patient ID, age, gender, collection date, received date and sample testing date are required. In contrast, other form fields may be optional depending on the information available or subject to change in future versions.

3.2 Entering Historical CD4 Results

This section lets you enter older CD4, reflex sCrAg and TB LAM results to build comprehensive patient histories. The process mirrors the ongoing entry section above, adding a dispatch date field.

Please note: The administrator needs to enable retrospective entry for specific periods upon request. If you need this capability, check with the administrator to ensure it is activated (support@mail.fahariyajamii.org).

This section lets you maintain accurate patient records for timely clinical decision-making. The following guide sections will provide instructions on effectively utilizing viewing CD4 results features. Let’s continue exploring LabPulse!

4. Viewing Results
{% elif section == "viewing_results" %}

The CD4 and sCrAg results section comprehensively looks at all results entered into LabPulse. This powerful section offers various tools and visualizations to help you analyze and understand the data. Let’s explore the key features.

4.1 Using the Filters

The 18 customizable filters let you refine your search and zoom in on specific results. You can apply filters individually or in groups depending on your needs. For example, you could:

  • Filter by a particular lab to see their results.
  • View results for an age group or sex to assess demographic impacts.
  • Find records from a given period.
  • Combine filters like lab, sex, and age range for targeted results.
4.2 Visualizations and Metrics

The result section includes helpful data visualizations:

  1. Summary Reports : Show the total CD4 samples processed and categorized into CD4>200 and CD4≤200. You can see the cascade for TB LAM and serum CrAg testing for the latter - positive, negative, and missing results. This quickly identifies patients needing intervention.
  2. Weekly Trend Charts : Display patterns and fluctuations in CD4 processing over time.
  3. Rejection Reasons : Help address issues and improve testing processes.
  4. Justification summaries : Compile explanations provided during data entry.
  5. Distribution Charts : Visualize CD4 counts across age, sex, and other factors.
  6. Lab Performance Metrics : Show the number of CD4/sCrAg reports processed per lab.
  7. Positivity Breakdowns : Provide distribution of positive TB LAM and serum CrAg results.
4.3 Robust Table and Download Features

The CD4 results table allows searching to organize and find data using filter at the top of the page. LabPulse also lets you download the following:

  1. All CD4 Results as PDFs for offline access and analysis.
  2. Lists of rejected samples and reasons.
  3. Records of positive TB LAM and serum CrAg to facilitate follow-up.
  4. Samples with missing results to prompt further action.

The table and downloads help streamline data management for better AHD screening and care.
